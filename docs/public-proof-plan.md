# AuthPilot AI Public Proof Plan

## Goal

Show that we are not just building a cool agent. We are building a company around a painful workflow that needs to exist now.

## Narrative

Use one clear public story everywhere:

AuthPilot AI turns live payer websites into submission-ready decisions for specialty clinics.

## Three Content Pillars

### 1. Pain

Show how bad the workflow is today:

- staff manually read payer policy pages
- staff compare rules with notes by hand
- staff search payer websites for the correct route
- mistakes are discovered too late

### 2. Product

Show the real workflow:

- TinyFish visits live payer pages
- the app extracts requirements
- the readiness engine compares chart evidence
- the product returns the next payer route and operator packet

### 3. Progress

Show company momentum:

- design partner calls
- product improvements
- real run metrics
- pilot learnings

## 14-Day Public Build Cadence

### Week 1

- Day 1: announce the wedge and problem
- Day 3: share a real live run with proof panel visible
- Day 5: post one workflow insight from discovery calls
- Day 7: share what changed in the product because of feedback

### Week 2

- Day 9: show run history, observability, or case packet improvements
- Day 11: post one sharp before-and-after workflow comparison
- Day 13: share design partner or pilot momentum
- Day 14: ship Demo Day video teaser or strongest clip

## X Post Templates

### Product Reveal

Prior auth teams still waste time reading payer policy pages by hand before they even start submission work.

We built AuthPilot AI to turn live payer websites into:

- readiness decisions
- missing-evidence detection
- exact payer routing

TinyFish handles the live web work.

### Live Run Proof

A real AuthPilot AI run:

- opened live payer policy page
- extracted evidence requirements
- matched chart support
- found correct provider precert route

This is browser-native clinic ops software, not a chatbot.

### Customer Insight

One thing we keep hearing from clinics:

The time sink is not only the submission itself. It is the manual payer research before the submission starts.

That is the bottleneck we are attacking first with AuthPilot AI.

## Demo Day Content Rules

- show live run IDs and proof
- show the artifact and operator packet
- say "specialty clinics" instead of "everyone"
- say "submission readiness and routing" instead of "full prior auth automation"
- never overclaim beyond what the product actually does

## Public Proof Dashboard

Track and share these numbers internally every week:

- live runs completed
- average run duration
- percentage of runs with usable routing output
- number of discovery calls
- number of active design partner conversations
- number of public posts shipped

## What To Avoid

- generic AI agent hype
- vague claims about healthcare transformation
- screenshots without business context
- polished videos that hide live execution
- posting product updates with no explanation of why they matter
