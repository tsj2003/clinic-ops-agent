# AuthPilot AI Design Partner Kit

## Goal

Land the first 2 design partners in spine, ortho, or pain clinics and get them to use the product weekly.

## Ideal Design Partner Profile

- 5 to 50 providers
- meaningful prior auth volume each week
- multiple commercial payers
- staff currently doing policy research by hand
- willing to test a new workflow if it saves time before submission

## Best Personas To Contact

- revenue cycle manager
- practice administrator
- prior auth lead
- surgery scheduler manager
- referral authorization supervisor

## Founding Pilot Offer

### What We Say

AuthPilot AI helps your staff decide whether a case is ready before they spend time inside a payer portal. It reads live payer policy pages, compares requirements against chart evidence, and identifies the right next payer route.

### Pilot Scope

- 30-day pilot
- one specialty workflow
- one to two staff users
- weekly review call
- live tuning of payer requirements and output packet

### Pilot Price

- ask target: $2,500
- acceptable range: $2,000 to $5,000 depending on volume and support load

If they resist, we can reduce scope before we reduce price.

## Pilot Success Criteria

We only call a pilot successful if we can show at least two of the following:

- measurable minutes saved per case
- fewer avoidable submissions
- clearer staff routing to the correct payer channel
- repeat weekly usage by at least one staff member

## Discovery Call Script

### Opening

"We are building a workflow for specialty clinics that cuts the manual payer research that happens before prior authorization work starts. I want to understand how your team handles that today and whether this is painful enough to matter."

### Questions

1. Walk me through what happens before someone actually starts a prior auth submission.
2. Which payers or procedures create the most confusion for your team?
3. How do staff know whether the chart has enough evidence before they submit?
4. Where do staff go to find the correct policy and contact route today?
5. What usually causes wasted time or rework?
6. How often do you find out too late that something is missing?
7. If this were solved, what would improve first: speed, fewer denials, or less staff load?

### Close

"We already have a working live-web prototype. If we showed you a version tailored to your specialty and a few of your common payer paths, would you be open to a short pilot?"

## Objection Handling

### "We already have prior auth software."

That is fine. We are not replacing your entire prior auth stack. We are solving the research and readiness bottleneck before staff get into the submission workflow.

### "We do this manually today."

That is exactly the point. If the workflow is manual, repetitive, and browser-heavy, that is where we can create value fastest.

### "We do not want to change too much."

The pilot can start as a lightweight pre-submission step with one staff user. No full workflow replacement required.

### "We need integrations."

We do not need to start there. The pilot can run as a standalone workflow first, and we can prioritize integrations once we prove repeated value.
